Baba Painter

Baba Painter is a serious interactive art installation serving two purposes: first, as a training and experimental restoration installation for restoration experts to practice and hone their skills, and secondly, for the creation of fun, social networking activities.

Following years of restoration, Baba House on Neil Road, Singapore, managed by NUS Museum, has been restored to its former glory, reflecting the sophistication, opulence, intricacies and charm of the Peranakan culture. Some artifacts however, like the Phoenix and Peony remain unrestored because the restoration team felt that they had neither the skill, expertise or knowledge to restore it. Members of the general public, children, students and scholars, alike, are invited to engage in the restoration activity and upload their creations to a shared internet site where others can view and critique their creations in a fun and social way.
